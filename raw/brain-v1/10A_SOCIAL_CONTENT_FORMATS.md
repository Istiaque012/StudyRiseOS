# 10A · SOCIAL CONTENT FORMATS — Reusable Post Templates

<!-- docnav -->
> **Docs ·** folder map [README.md](README.md) · part of the [StudyRise docs index](../README.md) · repo index [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md)

> **What this file is.** A library of ready-to-use post templates for every platform and format. Each template includes the structure, a worked example, audience and pillar tags, and an AI image generation prompt so the visual is plug-and-play. Pick a template, fill in the blanks, generate the visual, post.

> **Depends on:** `10_SOCIAL_PLAYBOOK.md` (voice rules, pillar definitions, platform specs, hashtag clusters), `02_BRAND_VOICE.md` (tone, banned phrases), `11_PRICING_MODEL.md` (CTA language), `07_IMAGE_PROTOCOL.md` (brand visual standards), Brand Kit v2.2 (colours, typography).

> **Video constraint:** Istiaque does not appear on camera. All video templates use screen recordings, voiceover-over-visuals, animated text, or motion graphics — never face-to-camera.

---

## How to Use This File

1. Decide what you want to post (pillar + audience).
2. Find the matching template below.
3. Fill in the bracketed `[PLACEHOLDERS]`.
4. Generate the visual using the included AI image prompt.
5. Post according to the platform specs in `10_SOCIAL_PLAYBOOK.md` §5.5 (dimensions) and §8 (hashtags).

Every template is tagged:

- **Platforms:** Which channels it works on.
- **Pillar:** Study Technique / Exam Strategy / Product in Action / Community & Credibility (from `10_SOCIAL_PLAYBOOK.md` §4).
- **Audience:** [EXAM], [UNI], [MBBS-BD], or [ALL].
- **Funnel stage:** Awareness (cold) / Consideration (warm) / Conversion (hot).

### Template C1: The Step-by-Step Guide (5–7 slides)

A numbered walkthrough teaching one technique or process.

**Platforms:** Instagram (primary), LinkedIn, Facebook
**Pillar:** Study Technique or Exam Strategy
**Audience:** [ALL] or segment-specific
**Funnel stage:** Awareness → Consideration

**Slide structure:**

| Slide | Content | Design |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Cover | Title question or bold statement. "How to plan 4 months for AMC MCQ" | Navy background, large white Inter Bold title, cyan accent line |
| 2–6 — Steps | One step per slide. Number + short heading + 2–3 line explanation | Navy background, step number in large cyan, text in white |
| 7 — Summary + CTA | Recap the steps as a numbered list. CTA: "Try StudyRise free — link in bio." | Purple-to-navy gradient, white text, logo |

**Caption structure:**
```
[Slide 1 title repeated as the opening line — identical wording.]

[One sentence of context — why this matters.]

Save this and come back when you're ready to start.

[CTA — "Try StudyRise free" if the guide relates to a feature.]

[3–5 hashtags]
```

**Design rules:**
- One idea per slide. No walls of text.
- Maximum 40 words per slide (not counting the heading).
- Consistent layout across all slides — same text position, same font sizes, same background.
- Slide numbers visible (top-left or integrated into the heading).

**AI image prompt (per slide — adapt the text for each):**

## Part 5 — Facebook Group Posts

### Template F1: The Helpful Answer (for sharing in groups)

A post designed to be shared in relevant Facebook groups (AMC preparation groups, MBBS batch groups, university study groups). The value comes first; the product mention is secondary.

**Platforms:** Facebook (groups)
**Pillar:** Study Technique or Exam Strategy
**Audience:** Segment-specific — match the group
**Funnel stage:** Awareness → Consideration

**Structure:**
```
[Opening — address the group's common question directly.
 "A lot of people in this group ask about [topic]. Here's
 what I've found works:"]

[The answer — 3–5 concrete, actionable points. Numbered.
 Each point is 1–2 sentences.]

[Optional — brief personal context: "I'm building a study
 planner (StudyRise) and this is based on what our users
 tell us works." — one sentence only.]

[Closing — invite discussion: "What's worked for you?"]

[NO hashtags in groups. NO link in the post body unless
 the group explicitly allows it. Put the link in a comment
 if needed.]
```

**Rules:**
- Follow the group's rules. If self-promotion isn't allowed, don't mention StudyRise.
- The post must be genuinely helpful even if every brand reference is removed.
- Never copy-paste the same post across multiple groups. Adapt the opening for each group's specific context.
- [MBBS-BD] group posts: use the restrained tone from `10_SOCIAL_PLAYBOOK.md` §5.3. No gamification, no pressure framing.

### Template ST1: The Article Launch Story Sequence (3–5 stories)

A sequence of Instagram/Facebook stories promoting a new blog article.

**Platforms:** Instagram Stories, Facebook Stories
**Pillar:** Any (matches the article's pillar)
**Audience:** Matches the article's audience
**Funnel stage:** Consideration → Conversion

**Story sequence:**

| Story | Content | Design |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Hook | "[Question or bold statement from the article's H1]" + "New on the blog ↓" | Text on navy background, large white type |
| 2 — Key insight | One takeaway from the article — the most shareable finding | Text card or quote graphic |
| 3 — Visual | OG image from the article, or a key diagram/screenshot | Full-bleed image |
| 4 — CTA | "Read the full guide ↓" + link sticker to the article URL | Text on brand gradient + link sticker |
| 5 (optional) — Poll | "Have you tried [technique from the article]?" Yes / Not yet | Poll sticker on navy background |

**Rules:**
- Stories expire in 24 hours — they're ephemeral. Don't over-design.
- Add the article to a Highlight collection ("Blog" or "Guides") after posting.
- Link stickers always point to the full article URL, never the homepage.

## Part 7 — Article-to-Social Adaptation Cheat Sheet

When a new blog article is published, use this quick reference to know which templates to deploy. Full sequencing and timing rules live in `10B_SOCIAL_CALENDAR.md`.

| Article type | Facebook | Instagram | YouTube | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Study technique guide | F1 (group post) + S3 (tip card) | C1 (step carousel) + V2 (animated tip) | V2 (Short) | L2 (data insight) |
| Exam strategy article | F1 (group post) + S1 (stat) | C1 (step carousel) + V4 (plan walkthrough) | V3 (data story) or V4 | L2 or L3 (take) |
| Product walkthrough | S4 (before/after) | V1 (screen recording Reel) | V5 (full tutorial) + V1 (Short) | Skip or L1 (build update) |
| Comparison / listicle | S2 (myth vs fact) | C2 (comparison carousel) or C3 (listicle) | V3 (data story Short) | L2 (data insight) |
| MBBS-BD specific | F1 (BD group post) | Skip (low priority audience) | V1 or V5 (walkthrough) | Skip |
