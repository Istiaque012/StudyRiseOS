Not every audience lives everywhere. This map drives where content goes.

**[MBBS-BD] — Bangladeshi MBBS students**
- **Primary:** Facebook (groups, pages, Messenger). This audience lives on Facebook — it's the dominant social platform in Bangladesh. Facebook groups for specific medical colleges and BMDC batches are the primary discovery channel.

## 3. Voice Adaptation per Platform

The five voice pillars (Structured, Momentum-Driven, Trustworthy, Quietly Ambitious, Intelligent) are constant. But the *register* shifts per platform.

### 3.1 Facebook

**Register:** Warm, peer-level, community-oriented.

Facebook is where StudyRise talks *with* people, not *at* them. Posts should feel like they belong in a study group chat — helpful, direct, a little conversational, but never sloppy.

- **Tone:** Second person. Friendly but not performative. "Here's something that helped" over "YOU NEED TO SEE THIS."
- **Length:** Medium. 80–200 words for text posts. Longer is fine if the content earns it.
- **Engagement style:** Ask one genuine question at the end. Not "What do you think? 🤔" — ask something specific: "How many hours are you doing per day right now?" or "Which phase are you in?"
- **Bangla mixing ([MBBS-BD] posts only):** Natural code-switching is fine where it reflects how this audience actually speaks. Don't force it. Don't write full Bangla posts unless Istiaque explicitly requests one.
- **Group sharing:** When sharing in Facebook groups, the post must be genuinely helpful first. The product mention is secondary. Follow group rules. Never spam.

**Facebook don'ts:**
- No hashtag walls. 0–3 hashtags maximum.
- No engagement bait ("Tag a friend who needs this!").
- No emoji-heavy formatting (🔥💯🙌). One or two purposeful emoji max.

### 3.2 Instagram

**Register:** Visual-first, concise, aspirational-but-real.

Instagram is a visual platform. The image or video does the work; the caption supports it. StudyRise's Instagram leans into the "structured, aesthetic study planning" angle — clean layouts, real screenshots, study setup shots.

- **Tone:** Slightly lighter than other platforms. Still second person, still coach-voice, but more concise.
- **Caption length:** Varies by format. Reels: 1–3 sentences + CTA. Carousels: 2–4 sentences framing the slides. Single image: 3–5 sentences.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 per post, in the caption body or first comment. Relevant and specific, never a wall.
- **Stories:** Used for time-sensitive content (exam registration deadlines, new article launches, polls). Casual and ephemeral — don't over-design.
- **Reels:** The primary growth format in 2026. See `10A_SOCIAL_CONTENT_FORMATS.md` for script frameworks.

**Instagram don'ts:**
- No text-only posts. Every post needs a visual.
- No corporate-speak. This is where the [UNI] voice shines — practical, peer-level, clean.
- No repurposed TikTok content with watermarks. Instagram's algorithm penalises this — shoot natively or remove watermarks and add original elements.

### 3.3 YouTube

**Register:** Knowledgeable peer explaining something clearly.

YouTube serves two formats: **Shorts** (under 60 seconds, vertical, algorithm-driven discovery) and **long-form** (tutorials, walkthroughs, exam strategy deep dives). Both follow the same voice but different structures.

- **Shorts tone:** Hook-first. Deliver one insight in under 60 seconds. Conversational but efficient. No "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel" — start with the value.
- **Long-form tone:** Ali Abdaal register — evidence-first, structured, no fluff, occasional dry humour. Chapters are mandatory.
- **Titles:** SEO-optimised. Include the primary keyword. No ALL CAPS clickbait. Acceptable: "How I'd Plan 4 Months for AMC MCQ (Week by Week)". Unacceptable: "THIS Study Plan Will CHANGE YOUR LIFE!! 🤯"
- **Descriptions:** First 2 lines are the hook (visible before "Show more"). Include the article link if the video companions a blog post. Include timestamps. End with the CTA link.
- **Thumbnails:** Clean, readable at mobile size. Brand palette (navy/purple/cyan). Text overlay: 4–6 words maximum.

**YouTube don'ts:**
- No videos without a clear single takeaway. If you can't state the viewer benefit in one sentence, the video isn't ready.
- No unstructured rambling. Script or bullet-point every video before recording.

### 3.4 LinkedIn

**Register:** Professional, data-informed, founder-building-in-public.

LinkedIn is where StudyRise talks to the professional layer — IMG doctors navigating career transitions, medical educators, edtech peers, potential collaborators. This is the only platform where founder voice (Istiaque's perspective) is the default.

- **Tone:** First person (Istiaque's voice) or brand voice — never switch mid-post. Professional but not corporate. Data and evidence land well here. Personal narrative works if it's relevant, not performative.
- **Length:** LinkedIn rewards longer posts (800–1,500 characters perform best). Use line breaks generously — LinkedIn's feed collapses long text.
- **Format:** Lead with a strong opening line (the first ~150 characters are visible before "see more"). One idea per post. End with a question or a concrete takeaway.
- **Content types that work:** Build-in-public updates (features shipped, growth milestones, lessons learned), data insights (exam statistics, study technique research), professional takes on medical education.

**LinkedIn don'ts:**
- No motivational platitudes. "Hard work pays off 💪" is not a LinkedIn post.
- No emoji-heavy formatting. Professional register.
- No hashtag walls. 3–5 relevant hashtags at the bottom, never inline.
- No direct selling. Product mentions are contextual, never the point of the post.

## 4. Content Pillars for Social

Every social post maps to one of these pillars. If a post doesn't fit a pillar, it doesn't get posted.

### Pillar 1 — Study Technique (40% of posts)

Evidence-based study methods. Spaced repetition, active recall, interleaving, Pomodoro technique, planning methods. This is the largest pillar because it serves all three audiences and drives the most organic reach.

**Examples:** "The 20-card morning routine that actually sticks," "Why your study plan fails by week 3 (and how to fix it)," "Spaced repetition intervals explained in 60 seconds."

**Audience:** [ALL]

### Pillar 2 — Exam Strategy (25% of posts)

Exam-specific content. AMC MCQ strategy, PLAB 1 preparation, BMDC exam structure, university finals planning. Always fact-checked against authoritative sources.

**Examples:** "AMC MCQ: how many questions per day is realistic?" "BMDC Phase 1 subjects — what you're actually assessed on," "3 things I'd change if I sat the AMC again."

**Audience:** [EXAM] or [MBBS-BD] — never mix exam types in one post.

### Pillar 3 — Product in Action (20% of posts)

How StudyRise works. Screen recordings, feature walkthroughs, before/after planning screenshots (anonymised). This pillar exists to show, not tell.

**Examples:** "Setting up a 4-month AMC plan in StudyRise (screen recording)," "Here's what your analytics dashboard shows after 2 weeks," "How the auto-rescheduler handles a missed day."

**Audience:** [ALL] — but the example shown should match the platform's primary audience.

**Rules:**
- Never show user data that isn't dummy/demo data or explicitly consented.
- Screenshots use the real app — never mockups that misrepresent the current state.
- CTA is "Try StudyRise free" — follow `11_PRICING_MODEL.md` exactly.

### Pillar 4 — Community & Credibility (15% of posts)

Founder updates, user stories (when available), behind-the-scenes development, study motivation that passes the wince test. This pillar builds trust and the human layer.

**Examples:** "Why I built StudyRise (the 2am study-plan spreadsheet story)," milestone updates ("500 users this week"), reshared user feedback (with permission).

**Audience:** [ALL]

**Rules:**
- User stories require explicit permission before posting.
- Growth numbers are only shared when they're real and verifiable. No vanity metrics.
- "Building in public" is honest — share struggles alongside wins.

## 5. Non-Negotiable Social Copy Rules

These override everything else. Memorise them.

### 5.1 Pricing and CTA language

All social copy follows `11_PRICING_MODEL.md` and `02_BRAND_VOICE.md` §7. Quick reference:

| ✅ Always OK | ❌ Never |
|---|---|
| "Try StudyRise free" | "Free trial" |
| "Start free — no card required." | "Premium" |
| "Free for 30 days — every feature, no card required." | "Unlock" |
| "Basic features free forever." | "Upgrade" (in social — only OK on pricing page) |
| Link to `https://www.studyrise.app/?auth=register` | Link to bare `studyrise.app` |

### 5.2 Banned words and phrases (social-specific additions)

The full banned list lives in `02_BRAND_VOICE.md` §7.2. These additional items are banned specifically in social copy:

| Phrase | Why |
|---|---|
| "Link in bio" (as the entire CTA) | Lazy. Tell them what they'll find: "Free study planner — link in bio." |
| "Drop a 🔥 if you agree" | Engagement bait. Violates Trustworthy pillar. |
| "Tag someone who needs this" | Engagement bait. |
| "No one is talking about this" | They are. And it's an AI tell. |
| "This changed everything" | Hyperbolic. Violates Trustworthy pillar. |
| "STOP scrolling" | Aggressive. Incompatible with calm-coach voice. |
| "Life hack" / "Study hack" | On the banned list (file 02). |

### 5.3 [MBBS-BD] social tone

The restrained tone from `02_BRAND_VOICE.md` carries into social fully:

- No gamification language (streaks, leaderboards, "crush it").
- No pressure framing ("Don't fall behind," "Time is running out").
- No imported Western study-influencer clichés ("Grind mode," "Beast mode," "No excuses").
- Warm, calm, locally relevant. These students face documented high stress. Every post should leave them feeling more capable, not more anxious.
- Bangla code-switching is natural and welcome. Full Bangla posts only if Istiaque writes or approves the copy.

### 5.4 Medical and exam facts

- **Never guess.** Every exam fact (pass rates, registration dates, fees, format changes) must be verified against the authoritative source (AMC, GMC, ECFMG, BMDC) before posting.
- **BMDC curriculum facts** are sourced exclusively from `FEATURES.md`. If the fact isn't there, flag it and ask — don't improvise.
- **Date-sensitive content** (exam registration deadlines, policy changes) must include the verification date: "As of June 2026" or "Confirmed from the AMC website, June 2026."

### 5.5 Image and visual standards

- **Brand palette:** Navy (#0D1B3E), purple (#7C3AED), cyan (#06B6D4), blue (#2563EB). See Brand Kit §3.
- **Typography:** Inter for all text overlays. See Brand Kit §4.
- **Logo placement:** StudyRise logo (gradient version) in the corner of branded graphics — never as the focal point.
- **Image dimensions per platform:**

| Platform | Feed post | Story/Reel | Carousel slide |
|---|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 1200×630 | 1080×1920 | 1080×1080 |
| Instagram | 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait) | 1080×1920 | 1080×1080 |
| YouTube | Thumbnail: 1280×720 | Short: 1080×1920 | N/A |
| LinkedIn | 1200×627 | N/A | 1080×1080 |

- **No stock photos.** Use real screenshots, illustrations, or Canva-generated branded graphics. Generic stock images (people pointing at laptops, graduation caps) damage the Trustworthy pillar.

## 6. Platform Algorithm Notes (2026)

These are current as of June 2026. Review quarterly and update.

### 6.1 Facebook

- Algorithm favours meaningful interactions (comments, shares) over passive likes.
- Video posts (especially Reels) get broader distribution than static images.
- Posts in groups get more organic reach than page posts.
- Link posts (with URL previews) get lower reach than native content — put the link in the first comment for group posts, or use it in the post body for page posts only when the click is the goal.

### 6.2 Instagram

- Reels are the primary discovery mechanism. 50% of time spent on the platform is on Reels.
- The algorithm prioritises: watch time > shares/sends > saves > comments > likes.
- Reels between 60–90 seconds receive the highest engagement when retention is strong.
- The hook must land by the 1.5-second mark. 50% of viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds.
- 3–5 hashtags outperform hashtag walls. Hashtags are categorisation signals, not discovery levers.
- Instagram's "Originality Score" penalises cross-posted content with watermarks from other platforms. Shoot natively.
- Best posting times (general): 7–9 AM and 11 AM–1 PM local audience time zone.
- Creators who post Reels regularly gain followers approximately 25% faster than those who don't.

### 6.3 YouTube

- **Shorts:** Algorithm is aggressive at pushing to non-subscribers. Completion rate and replay rate are the primary signals. Search-based discovery gives Shorts a longer tail than Reels or TikTok — views can compound for months.
- **Long-form:** Click-through rate (thumbnail + title) and average view duration are the two metrics that matter most. A 10-minute video with 50% average view duration outperforms a 20-minute video with 25%.
- Custom thumbnails are supported on Shorts — use them. A strong thumbnail can double tap-through.
- YouTube is the most search-driven of the four platforms. Titles and descriptions should include target keywords.

### 6.4 LinkedIn

- The algorithm rewards "dwell time" — how long people spend reading your post.
- Posts with strong opening lines (first 150 characters) get higher "see more" click-through.
- Comments in the first hour significantly boost distribution.
- Native documents (PDF carousels) get strong engagement.
- External links in the post body reduce reach. Put the link in the first comment if the primary goal is engagement, in the post body if the primary goal is clicks.

## 7. Posting Cadence — Minimum Viable Schedule

This is the floor for a solo-operator schedule. If you can do more, scale the [ALL] pillar first (study techniques), because it has the broadest reach.

| Platform | Posts per week | Format split |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 3–4 | 2 text/image posts, 1 video/Reel, 1 article share |
| Instagram | 3–5 | 2 Reels, 1 carousel, 1 static post, Stories as needed |
| YouTube | 1–2 | 1 Short per week minimum; 1 long-form per month minimum |
| LinkedIn | 2–3 | Text posts with occasional document (PDF carousel) |

**Batching rule:** Record all Reels/Shorts in one session. Write all captions for the week in one sitting. Don't context-switch between creating and posting.

**Consistency over volume.** Three good posts per week beat seven mediocre ones. The algorithm rewards regularity, not volume.

## 9. Cross-Posting Rules

Repurposing content across platforms is efficient, but each platform has norms that must be respected.

### 9.1 What can be cross-posted

- **Article promotions:** Adapt the angle per platform. Facebook gets a longer excerpt with a question. Instagram gets a carousel or Reel. LinkedIn gets a professional-angle text post. YouTube gets a companion Short or long-form video.
- **Study tips:** A single study tip can become a Facebook text post, an Instagram Reel, a YouTube Short, and a LinkedIn post — but each version is written natively for the platform, not copy-pasted.
- **Data insights:** A single stat or research finding can be used across platforms with different framing.

### 9.2 What must NOT be cross-posted

- **Video files with watermarks.** Never post a Reel with a TikTok watermark, or vice versa. Instagram and YouTube both penalise this.
- **Identical captions across platforms.** Adapt the length, tone, and CTA for each channel.
- **Platform-specific features.** Don't reference Instagram Stories on LinkedIn. Don't use LinkedIn-style paragraph breaks on Instagram.

### 9.3 Cross-posting workflow

1. Create the core idea (one sentence: what's the insight?).
2. Write the primary version for the platform where this idea will perform best.
3. Adapt to secondary platforms — adjust length, tone, format, and CTA.
4. Schedule or post. (Batching preferred — see §7.)

## 10. UTM and Tracking

Every social post that links to studyrise.app must use UTM parameters for GA4 tracking.

### 10.1 UTM structure

```
https://www.studyrise.app/?auth=register
  &utm_source={platform}
  &utm_medium=social
  &utm_campaign={campaign_name}
  &utm_content={post_identifier}
```

| Parameter | Values |
|---|---|
| `utm_source` | `facebook`, `instagram`, `youtube`, `linkedin` |
| `utm_medium` | `social` (organic) or `paid_social` (ads — see `10C_PAID_PROMOTION.md`) |
| `utm_campaign` | Descriptive slug: `amc-article-launch`, `study-tips-series`, `mbbs-awareness` |
| `utm_content` | Optional. Differentiates A/B variants: `carousel-v1`, `reel-hook-a` |

### 10.2 Shortened links

Use UTM-tagged links directly. If a URL shortener is needed (Instagram bio, YouTube description), use a branded shortener or Bitly — never obscure the destination.

## 11. Community Engagement Rules

Social media is a two-way channel. These rules govern how StudyRise responds.

### 11.1 Response priorities

| Type | Response time target | Who responds |
|---|---|---|
| Direct question about the product | Within 4 hours | Istiaque |
| Study-related question in comments | Within 24 hours | Istiaque (or brain-drafted reply for review) |
| Positive feedback | Within 24 hours | Istiaque — brief, genuine, not performative |
| Negative feedback or bug report | Within 4 hours | Istiaque — acknowledge, take to DM |
| Spam or irrelevant comments | Delete or hide | Istiaque |

### 11.2 Comment voice

- Same calm-coach voice as the rest of the brand.
- Be genuinely helpful. A good comment reply is worth more than a good post.
- Never argue in comments. Acknowledge, clarify if needed, move on.
- Never promote in others' comment sections unless it's directly relevant and genuinely helpful.
